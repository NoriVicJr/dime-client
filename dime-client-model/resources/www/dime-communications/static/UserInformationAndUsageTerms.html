User Information and Usage Terms for the di.me trial at  IV Curso De Verano AMETIC-UPM

Thank you very much for trying out the di.me system on the IV Curso de Verano AMETIC-UPM! With this user study, we evaluate a first working prototype of our research project. The study aims to approve the system’s performance, usability, and acceptance by end-users.

For us it is of high value that you try out this prototype and give us your feedback!

Who we are: the di.me European research project led by Fraunhofer

The di.me project is a research project developing a new technology for personal information management, funded by the European Union (FP7/2007- 2013, n° 257787). The project is led by Fraunhofer Gesellschaft für angewandte Forschung, a major German non-profit research organization. Fraunhofer, AMETIC, University of Siegen, and five other public research institutions and private companies (see www.dime-project.eu) are partners of the di.me project (henceforth “the di.me consortium” and “we”).

The di.me system: a research prototype for advanced, privacy-ensuring social communication

The di.me system that we ask you to try out is a working prototype for a system organizing advanced, privacy-ensuring social communication. It is specifically set up for the IV Curso de Verano AMETIC-UPM.

The following components of the di.me system consists of the following technical components:

1) a personal server for each user. The personal server of one user is technically separated from the personal servers of other users. The personal servers run on web servers provided by the University of Siegen as partner of the di.me consortium.

2) the Android application (client) that you have installed on your smartphone connects to your personal server.

3) the di.me services that are called to perform functions that cannot be executed on your personal server. These services run on web servers provided by the partners of the di.me consortium.

Your privacy

The di.me system stores personal data to realize the functionalities of the system and scientific analysis of anonymised log files. Data will not be used for other than the designated purpose, in particular not for any commercial purpose.

Where are the data stored?

All data entered or collected in di.me are stored on your personal server. Data in the Android application are transmitted to your personal server. The di.me services use anonymised data for specific functions: if you use rate the “places” a voting function in di.me to get personalized POI recommendations, your anonymised ratings are transferred. If you activate the function “share my location” on your Android phone, your location information from the Android phone is used for

to automatically detect and suggest to you at which place and situation you currently are, and what groups of people are nearby. Your current place, and situations are shown only to you. The groups of people nearby are shown to all people using the location sharing. All automatic suggestions are calculated on a di.me server that gets only your location information and a pseudonym.

Which data are stored?

The following data are used by the system:

1) the information you enter or upload to the system, including files or photos that you upload to the system

2) if you have agreed to that when registering to IV Curso de Verano AMETIC-UPM: information from the registration web page

3) if you connect di.me to external systems (e.g. LinkedIn or Twitter), data from there are copied to the di.me system

4) if you activate the function “share my location” on your Android phone: anonymized location data from your mobile phone

5) for scientific, anonymised analysis: information on what you do with the system (log files); see below

Deletion of data after four weeks

We will delete all personal data after four weeks when we delete the di.me system used for the IV Curso De Verano AMETIC-UPM, but will retain anonymised log files for scientific analysis (see below.)

Anonymized log files for scientific analysis

For our project, it is important to analyse how the system is used in order to optimize it. For that, log files are stored that we want to analyse for user actions. For our analysis, we will generate a anonymised version of these log files. All data that can be traced back to your identity will be deleted (like: name, company name, field of study, email address, any di.me user-names or passwords, personal documents, photos, etc.).

- respecting the rules of privacy and confidentiality prescribed by the law

- making no unauthorized access to data, or the IT infrastructure

- taking care to protect personal and sensitive data and avoid infringement of intellectual property

rights or other personal rights of any other user or third parties.
